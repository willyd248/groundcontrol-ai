The thesis

Ground operations are the last unautomated frontier in commercial aviation

Every major airport still relies on human controllers to coordinate ground vehicles, manage gate assignments, and sequence taxiing aircraft. The information exists — real-time positions, gate states, weather, runway status — but the dispatch decisions remain manual. GroundControl AI is a proof of concept that this layer can be fully automated.

01 Ingest real data. The live JFK tracker above pulls ADS-B positions for every aircraft. The same pipeline can ingest vehicle telemetry, gate sensors, and weather feeds.
02 Model the state. Build a real-time graph of the airport surface — which gates are occupied, which runways are active, which vehicles are available and where.
03 AI makes the dispatch decisions. Optimal vehicle routing, runway crossing approval, pushback sequencing — all computed in milliseconds. The simulation demonstrates this with 18 vehicle types and live dispatch logs.
04 Outcome: 10–25% reduction in aircraft turnaround time, elimination of ground incursion risk, and continuous optimization as conditions change.
